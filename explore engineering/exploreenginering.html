Why Choose Mechanical?

Mechanical Engineering is one of the oldest and evergreen branches of engineering, which has contributed to several innovations. It can be termed the "mother of all engineering.‚Äù Mechanical Engineering deals with concepts of thermal and fluid engineering, mechanics & structural analysis, design & development of refrigeration & air-conditioning systems, and manufacturing.

Below are the major domain areas in Mechanical Engineering:

Design

Mechanical engineers in this role are responsible for designing thermal systems, mechanical systems, or HVAC systems, applying principles of mechanics, thermodynamics, hydraulics, and computer-aided analysis.

Manufacturing and Production

In this role, mechanical engineers design, develop, test, and manufacture machinery and ensure its proper performance and efficiency.

Robotics & Mechatronics

Robotics and process automation have become crucial in industries, requiring mechanical engineers to design robotic systems and implement process automation.

Power and Metal Plants

Mechanical engineers are needed in thermal power plants due to their knowledge of thermodynamics and material science.

Quality Improvement and Assurance

Mechanical engineers work on designing quality standards, inspecting materials, and developing quality control systems.

Logistics and Supply Chain Management

Mechanical engineers play a role in analyzing and optimizing supply chain operations.

Installation & Maintenance

Mechanical engineers install and maintain machine parts in industrial locations.

Why Choose Electronics and telecommunication

Electronics and Telecommunications (EXTC) Engineering involves research, designing, developing, and testing of electronic equipment used in various communication systems. The EXTC Engineering a backbone of modern communication system that rules rocket science to telecommunication everything. Electronics and Communications engineers also conceptualize and oversee the manufacturing of communications and broadcast systems.

The branch deals with Basic Electronics, Solid state devices, Microprocessors/Microcontrollers, Analog/Digital communication, Analog Integrated circuits, Antenna and Wave Propagation, Microwave engineering, Satellite communication, Mobile communication and also includes advanced courses like 5G, IoT and Industrial automation. Looking into recent trends subjects and overall learning requisites the multidisciplinary environment, the EXTC curriculum is further strengthened by adding subjects like Data Structures and Algorithms, DBMS, Big Data Analytics.

Over the years, communication and electronics have crept in our lives like never before. This change in the technological field has ensured a bright path ahead for EXTC pursuers. With smartphones in hand and widespread opportunities to communicate, electronics and communication engineering is the next best thing to rule the global market.

Why Choose Computer Engineering?

Computer engineering integrates fields of computer science and electronic engineering to develop hardware and software. It covers areas such as artificial intelligence (AI), robotics, computer networks, and more.

Major domains in Computer Engineering include:

Quantum Computing

Integrating quantum behavior with classical computing for more efficient problem-solving.

Robotics

Design and automation of robotic systems.

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

AI involves building machines that can reason, learn, and act intelligently.

Cybersecurity

Preventing cyberattacks and protecting systems and data.

Cloud Computing

On-demand availability of computing resources over the internet.

Data Science

Extracting meaningful insights from data to help in decision-making.

Why Choose Electrical Engineering?

Electrical engineering focuses on the study, design, and application of equipment, devices, and systems that use electricity. It encompasses fields like power engineering, signal processing, and telecommunications.

Major domains in Electrical Engineering:

Power and Energy

Power engineers work on the generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity and related systems.

Instrumentation

Design of devices to measure physical quantities like pressure, flow, and temperature.

Signal Processing

Analysis and manipulation of signals for audio, telecommunications, and more.

Why Choose Civil Engineering?

Civil engineering focuses on the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment, including public works like roads, bridges, and buildings.

Major domains in Civil Engineering:

Coastal Engineering

Managing coastal areas and preventing erosion and flooding.

Construction Engineering

Planning and execution of construction projects, including transportation of materials.

Structural Engineering

Design and analysis of structures like buildings, bridges, and tunnels.
